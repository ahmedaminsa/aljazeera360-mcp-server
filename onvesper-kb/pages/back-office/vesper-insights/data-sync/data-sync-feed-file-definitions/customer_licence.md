# Customer\_Licence

The customer-licence offers all the details of all purchases attempted and converted by a customer to purchase a licence (ie: PPV, subscription, rental).

The customer-licence is a subscription that entitles a user to view specific content as permitted by the SKU associated with the licence. A customer can have one or multiple records in this table containing the latest state of each customer's licence.

This forms the basis to develop deeper analytics on what users are likely to sign up for, or deeper insights into the lifecycle of their subscription behaviors, and more.

### FAQ

This section attempts to answer frequent questions about data you find in this customer\_licence data sync feed

1. What is the logic around the 'is\_voluntary\_churn' field?
   * The 'is\_voluntary\_churn' field can contain three values (true, false, null): 'True' and 'False' for the licenses that are currently churned, and 'NULL' for the licenses that are currently active. 'is\_voluntary\_churn' is set to 'True' when the customer voluntarily deactivates or cancels the license. 'is\_voluntary\_churn' is set to 'False' when the platform revokes the licence due to something outside the control of the customer such as a failed payment renewal where the platform could not be collect payment as an example.
2. Why do past or old customer licence records suddenly appear in a new data sync feed that has never been sent before?
   1. This typically happens when users link an old IAP purchase receipt from the app. When this happens, the platform reads the purchase/licence in the receipt and records this. These will appear as revoked or expired licence status for unconfirmed purchases and recorded as not converted on our platform. If you look at only successfully converted purchases (ie: is\_successfully\_converted=true) this will have no impact on reporting numbers. If you look at unsuccessfully converted licences, such as determining purchases in-flight (waiting for payment confirmation), then you should include the order\_date in your queries to make sure you’re analyzing new orders (not the old).
